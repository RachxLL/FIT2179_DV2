Smoking Prevalence in Malaysia

Where smoking remains common, and how the national picture has changed

Smoking remains a major public health issue in Malaysia.
National prevalence has declined, but the pattern is uneven.
Some states recorded much higher smoking rates in 2023.

Men continued to smoke at far higher rates than women.
Smoking was also highest among middle-aged adults.
Education and income reveal further social differences.
Together, these patterns show progress, but uneven impact.

Education pattern
Primary education shows the highest smoking prevalence

Smoking was not evenly distributed.
The pattern changed across education groups.

Adults with primary education recorded 25.4%.
Adults with tertiary education recorded only 6.1%.

Education level is therefore important.

Data and Sources

Smoking data:
This page uses Malaysian smoking prevalence data.
It also uses e-cigarette and vape data.
The figures describe adults aged 15 and above.

Map data:
Malaysia state boundary data came from public TopoJSON.
State names were matched with smoking values.
This was done for educational visualisation use.

Reading the page:
The results show population-level smoking patterns.
They compare states, groups, products, and time.
They should not be read as single-factor causes.
