Smoking Prevalence in Malaysia

Geographic distribution by state, 2023

This visualization shows current smoking prevalence among adults aged 15 years and above across Malaysian states and federal territories. The darker the color intensity, the higher the smoking rate in that region. Understanding geographic variation is essential for targeted public health interventions and tobacco control policies.

Data and Methods

Source: Global Adult Tobacco Survey (GATS) Malaysia 2023, conducted by the Institute for Public Health in collaboration with the Disease Control Division, Ministry of Health Malaysia.

Definition: Current smokers are persons aged 15 years and above who report smoking tobacco daily or less than daily. Percentages represent the prevalence of current smoking among the total adult population in each state.

Interpretation: Geographic variation in smoking rates reflects differences in socioeconomic conditions, implementation of tobacco control policies, and cultural factors across regions.
