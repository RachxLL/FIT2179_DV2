Smoking Prevalence in Malaysia

Where smoking remains common, and how the national picture has changed

Smoking is still a major public health issue in Malaysia, even though national prevalence has declined over time. The pattern is not equal across the country. Some states recorded much higher smoking rates in 2023, while the national trend shows that progress has been gradual rather than sudden.

Smoking Rates Are Uneven Across States

In 2023, Sarawak recorded the highest smoking prevalence among Malaysian states, while Putrajaya recorded the lowest. The difference between states shows that smoking behaviour is not evenly spread across Malaysia. This suggests that public health attention may need to be more targeted in higher-prevalence areas.

Smoking Has Declined, But Slowly

Malaysia’s smoking prevalence has fallen compared with earlier survey years. The 2011 rate is an important comparison point because it shows where the country stood more than a decade ago. By 2023, the rate had dropped to 19.0%, but this still means smoking remained common among adults.

Data and Sources

Smoking data: This page uses Malaysian smoking prevalence data from national tobacco and health survey materials. The figures describe current smoking among adults aged 15 years and above.

Map data: Malaysia state boundary data was adapted from a public GitHub TopoJSON source for educational use. State names were matched with smoking prevalence values to show the geographic pattern.

Reading the page: The results show differences in smoking prevalence across states and over time. They describe population-level patterns and should not be read as proving that location alone causes smoking behaviour.
